Project Implementation

Prism Solutions implementation plan is a detailed, concrete plan, written in the checklist format. This list is compiled by our developers before starting work on each user story. It is a step-by-step instruction for performing each of the tasks prescribed by the engineer for himself or for other developers.

Then we look at each deliverable and define the series of tasks that must be completed to accomplish each one. For each task, we determine the amount of time it will take, the resources necessary, and who will be responsible for execution.

Project Management

Prism Solutions assign a Project Manager that is responsible for managing the progress of your project. The project manager will be one of your primary contacts and they will provide you with status updates, schedule meetings, communicate deadlines, and coordinate internal team meetings. Prism Solutions uses a combination of cloud based technologies and internal procedures to ensure that you and your team are kept up to date with project progress in real time.

Additionally, throughout the project we will hold weekly recurring meetings to provide you with updates on recent items completed and pending items to be completed. These meetings will include the project lead from your organization, your Prism Solutions Project Manager, and depending on the scope of your project additional Prism Solutions team members.

Steps of Project Management Plan

Business Process Review and Requirements Gathering

Analysis

Our first step in the project will be to assess in more detail of system strategies, current data, and how you work. Based on our extensive implementation experience, we know the crucial questions to ask to ensure we build an optimal project plan. We will spend time interviewing staff, reviewing your processes and data, and digging into the various systems you have in place. From those efforts we will be able to align the hours and services we have proposed here with the detail of exactly how we recommend the project proceed.


We will start this process with a brief kick-off meeting to introduce the project participants and review the plan. Then, we will conduct meetings with the organization’s stakeholders for this process to further clarify current processes and future goals. Stakeholders could include development or program staff, executive project sponsors, and the IT personnel managing the project. These meetings will be held online and, ideally, will be scheduled over the course of a few weeks. We anticipate two to three deep discovery meetings of 60 to 90 minutes each.

During these meetings, we will interview staff members to get a sense of:

  • Specifications of current IT structure
  • Completeness and structure of source data in various databases including Access
  • Precise functionality desired in new technology to include business process, notifications and day-to-day processes
  • Organizational priorities of desired features and functionality

From the Analyse and Design stage, Prism Solutions will create and request sign-off on a detailed requirements and solutions workbook, and a technical design document, if necessary, for the work efforts needed to successfully complete the project. (Note: upon completion of the Analyse and Design stage we may require revisiting estimates for build and data migration before proceeding with programming in the Build stage.)

Validate

We will implement and maintain a written Quality Control Plan (QCP) that will be based on our corporate Quality Management System (QMS) Procedures Manual. Our QMS Procedures Manual defines our quality management process, our approach to quality control, our quality control inspection system, our process for identifying and correcting deficiencies, how we promote customer relations and communications, and our guidelines for preparing QCPs. Our QCP will address the continuous improvement process; the procedures and responsibilities for scheduling, conducting, and documenting inspections; the process for identifying and correcting deficiencies; the procedures for root cause analysis to identify the root cause and root cause corrective action to prevent re-occurrence of discrepancies; the procedures for trend analysis; and the procedures for collecting and addressing customer feedback and complaints. Also, we will plan and attend post award conferences and other periodic meetings with the contracting activity to review our performance and to resolve any problems.
